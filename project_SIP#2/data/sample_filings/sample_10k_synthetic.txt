Item 1A. Risk Factors

Our business is subject to a number of risks, including risks related to global supply chain disruptions, which could materially affect our ability to source components on time and within budget. During the past fiscal year, transportation delays and semiconductor shortages increased our component costs by approximately 12%.

We also face risks related to foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations, particularly given that a significant portion of our manufacturing operations are located outside the United States. A strengthening of the U.S. dollar relative to other currencies could adversely affect our reported revenue when foreign sales are converted back to U.S. dollars.

Cybersecurity threats represent an increasing area of concern for our operations. We have invested $45 million in additional security infrastructure over the past two years, but there can be no assurance that these measures will prevent all future incidents.

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

Total revenue for the fiscal year increased by 8.3% to $2.1 billion, driven primarily by strong demand in our enterprise software segment. Gross margin declined slightly to 61.2%, compared to 63.5% in the prior year, primarily due to increased component costs discussed in the Risk Factors section above.

Operating expenses increased by 14% year-over-year, largely attributable to increased headcount in our research and development organization as we continue to invest in next-generation product lines. Management believes these investments position the company well for long-term growth, though near-term margin pressure is expected to continue into the next fiscal year.

Our Chief Financial Officer, Sarah Chen, noted during the earnings call that the company expects gross margin to stabilize in the range of 60-62% as supply chain conditions normalize.

Item 9A. Controls and Procedures

Management, including our Chief Executive Officer, David Ramirez, has evaluated the effectiveness of our disclosure controls and procedures. Based on this evaluation, management concluded that our disclosure controls and procedures were effective as of the end of the fiscal year covered by this report.
